As part of my senior design project my team and I conceptualized and prototyped a workholding arm. The goal of this project was to create a device that would be capable of holding 15 lbs objects in any position. The result of this was a 2-link arm with two ball joints at each end and an elbow joint in the middle. The system is default locked but can be unlocked with the press of a button. This drives a "screw" out of the elbow joint and back once the button is released. This is similar to how indicator stands operate. This project was the culmination of 4-year of undergraduate courses and involved many stress calculations given the large forces going through the device. It also required the use of CAD (Computer Aided Design) and introduced me to CAM (Computer Aided Manufacturing). Our device ended up placing second at the Malott Innovation Awards competition.


Skills: CAD, CAM, Statics, Dynamics, Arduino
